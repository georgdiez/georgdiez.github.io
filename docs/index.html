In the last few decades, we have witnessed a mathematical revolution in our conception of the living world. In particular, the biosciences are shifting from qualitative to quantitative as where experimental and computational techniques provide unprecedented amounts of data. We can now investigate the mysteries of "living processes" at a deeper level than ever before.
But as the amount of data available in the biosciences continues to grow, we face the exciting challenge of developing novel mathematical and computational concepts to effectively analyze and interpret this vast wealth of information. By rising to this challenge, we can unlock new insights and deepen our understanding of the complex processes underlying life.

Georg Diez

I am a PhD student in theoretical and computational biophysics at Moldyn Freiburg concerned with the modeling of Protein Dynamics.
